Boiler Water Quality Standards: A Guide to Maximizing Efficiency

  • Jun 22, 2024
  • By Shaw
  • 54 Views
  • 3 Comments

When it comes to industrial operations, maintaining the health and efficiency of your boiler system is paramount. A key factor in achieving this is ensuring that the water quality meets specific standards. Did you know that just a 1/4 inch of scale build-up can increase your fuel consumption by up to 20%? At Absolute Chemicals, we provide solutions that can help you reduce fuel costs by 10-20% and keep your boiler running smoothly.

Why Boiler Water Quality Matters

Water used in boiler systems often contains impurities such as hardness, magnesium, calcium, iron, silica, and dissolved gases. These impurities can lead to corrosion, scale formation, and reduced efficiency, ultimately shortening the lifespan of your equipment and increasing operational costs.

The Importance of Chemical Treatment

Our specialized chemicals are designed to eliminate these impurities, preventing scale and corrosion. This not only improves the efficiency of your boiler but also extends its operational life, reducing the need for frequent maintenance and unexpected shutdowns.

Recommended Boiler Water Parameters

To ensure optimal performance and steam quality, it is crucial to keep the water parameters within recommended limits. Below is a table outlining the maximum limits for boiler water based on different pressure levels:


Boiler Pressure (PSIG) Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) Alkalinity (ppm) Silica (ppm)
0 - 300 3500 700 125
301 - 450 2500 600 90
451 - 600 1500 500 75
601 - 750 1000 350 50
751 - 900 1500 300 28
901 - 1000 1250 250 8
1001 - 1500 1000 200 2.5
1501 - 2000 750 150 1.5

Maintaining these parameters helps ensure that your boiler operates efficiently, produces high-quality steam, and minimizes the risk of damage.

Benefits of Using Absolute Chemicals

By using Absolute Chemicals for your water treatment needs, you can:

  • Maximize Efficiency: Keep your boiler running at peak performance.
  • Extend Equipment Life: Reduce wear and tear on your system.
  • Lower Operational Costs: Decrease fuel consumption and maintenance expenses.
  • Increase Profitability: Optimize your system’s efficiency to save on energy and costs.
